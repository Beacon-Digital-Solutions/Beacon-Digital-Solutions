Our approach to your data

This is the foundation of every tool we create.

Your data stays with you

Your financial information lives on your devices and syncs only through your own private iCloud — Apple's encrypted database that we cannot read. There's no Beacon server: we never receive, store, or have access to any of it.

We do not ask for your data

You do not need to create an account with us. You do not need to upload your information. Optional features such as local import keep everything under your control on your device.

No tracking of any kind

Our tools do not track your activity, build profiles, or send data back to us. This is a deliberate design choice, not a setting you have to enable.

Built for households

Household Sharing — inviting your spouse to the same budget on their own Apple ID, peer-to-peer through iCloud — is on the roadmap for a future update. When it ships, it will follow the same rule as everything else: we never see your data.

You bring your own records

Import files from your own sources when you want to. We have no visibility into the data you choose to work with.

Protection by design

We build this way because we believe in looking after the people who use our tools. Your data is yours. We treat it that way.

Common questions

Is Thrive really free?

Yes. The core zero-based budgeting toolkit — budgets, transactions, imports, dashboard, annual review — is free forever, with no account and no ads. Premium ($8/mo) adds retirement, education, and real-estate planning tools, with a 7-day free trial in-app.

Does Thrive connect to my bank?

No — by design. You never hand over bank credentials. Import transactions from the CSV, PDF, or OFX files your bank already gives you, or optionally let Thrive read transaction-alert emails you receive. Nothing about your accounts ever touches our servers, because we don't have any.

Where is my financial data stored?

On your device, and synced between your Mac and iPhone through your own private iCloud (Apple's encrypted CloudKit). There is no company server, no account with us, and no analytics or tracking of any kind.

What does "zero-based budgeting" mean?

Every dollar of income gets assigned a job — giving, bills, savings, spending — until income minus assignments equals zero. It's the method behind envelope budgeting and apps like YNAB and EveryDollar, and it's the fastest way to know exactly where your money goes.

How is Thrive different from YNAB, Mint, or EveryDollar?

Three things: privacy (no bank linking, no company servers — your data never leaves your Apple devices and iCloud), a native Mac-first app that also runs on iPhone, and a stewardship worldview — Giving/Tithe is a first-class category, and the planning tools treat money as something you manage faithfully, not just optimize.

Is Thrive a Christian budgeting app?

Thrive is built around Christian stewardship — being faithful with what you've been given. Giving comes first in the default budget, and the monthly review is encouraging rather than shaming. That said, it's a full-featured budgeting app anyone can use.
